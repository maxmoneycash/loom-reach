# Research grounding for scenario catalogs (condensed, cited)

## Defense (DLA / Berry) — real anchors
- **DLA Troop Support C&T**: ~$1.9B/yr, 8,000+ items → **30,000+ line items once sized**; FFP IDIQs, 1 base + 4 option years, via DIBBS. (dla.mil, ncto.org)
- **"Size tariff"** = the actual military term for mandated size distributions (Army SB 10-523).
- **Lead-time pathology**: GAO NSIAD-94-64 — DOD C&T procurement lead ~**400 days vs 2–60 days** commercial; 26% of items had 10 YEARS of supply. (gao.gov/assets/nsiad-94-64.pdf)
- Real suppliers: **ADS Inc** (SOE TLS IDIQ, $33B ceiling, 6 awardees, 2021), **Propper** (ACU/OCP; $57M boots IDIQ 2025), **Crye Precision** (JPC plate carriers, Brooklyn), **Mystery Ranch** (SOCOM SPEAR packs 2016), **DRIFIRE/NSA + Massif** (Berry FR layers, Safe-to-Fly), **American Apparel Inc (Selma AL)** ($11–32M DLA sewing IDIQs).
- Contract shape to model: ~**240,000 hot-weather uniforms/yr @ ~$150/set** (~$37M, DLA 2021 solicitation), option-year qty uncertainty ±40%.
- Anduril runs a real gear/apparel store (gear.anduril.com). Epirus = counter-drone HPM ($250M Series D 2025, Anduril partner) — Anatar logo relationship UNVERIFIED; model as illustrative "FSR crew kit."

## Commercial disasters (cited numbers for in-app copy)
- **Nike FY23**: inventory $9.7B, **+44% YoY** (NA +65%), lead-time variance + double-ordering → markdown campaign, ~330bps GM hit. (SEC 8-K Q1 FY23)
- **Adidas Yeezy**: **€1.2B** stranded stock, €700M loss warning; recovered €750M via re-releases. (adidas-group.com)
- **Allbirds 2022**: apparel expansion flop — reserve $1.7M→$8.3M, ~$13M⚠ liquidation, $116.8M inventory. (IR)
- **Gap/Old Navy 2022 — THE size-curve case**: BODEQUALITY 0–28 in every store → over-bought extended sizes, stocked out of core sizes; **merch margin −5pts**, guidance pulled, CEO out. (SEC Q2 2022 8-K) [Do NOT cite "$200M writedown" — not found.]
- **VF Corp**: inventories +88% incl. $510M in-transit jump. **H&M 2018**: $4.3B unsold, op profit −62%. **Lululemon 2013**: sheer-Luon recall pulled ~17% of bottoms → stockout side.

## Quick response (cited)
- **Zara** (HBS case, secondary⚠): pre-season commit **15–20% vs industry 45–60%**; in-season 40–50% vs ~0–20%; ~15-day reactive lead; markdowns ~15% vs ~35%; unsold ~10% vs 17–20%.
- **Shein**: test batches **100–200 units**, reorder on threshold; supplier pivot 3–7 days; design-to-shelf 7–14 days.⚠
- **Sport Obermeyer / Fisher-Raman** (HBR 1994, OR 1996): accurate response raised profits **~50–100%**; new-item pre-season forecast error typically **50–100%**.
